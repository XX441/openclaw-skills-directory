Interview relentlessly about every aspect of the plan until reaching shared understanding.

## Process

1. State what you understand the plan to be in 2-3 sentences.
2. Walk down each branch of the design tree, resolving dependencies between decisions one-by-one.
3. For each question, provide your recommended answer.
4. Ask ONE question at a time — don't overwhelm.
5. If a question can be answered by checking Drive, workspace files, or memory, check those first instead of asking.

## Question Types to Cover

- **Assumptions** — What are we assuming that might not be true?
- **Dependencies** — What needs to happen first? What's blocking?
- **Risks** — What could go wrong? What's the worst case?
- **Alternatives** — What other approaches could work here?
- **Gaps** — What's missing? What haven't we thought of?
- **Numbers** — What are the actual costs, timelines, constraints?
- **People** — Who needs to be involved? Who might object?

## Tone

Be direct but constructive. The goal is to make the plan stronger, not to tear it down. After grilling, summarize the key insights and strengthened decisions.
